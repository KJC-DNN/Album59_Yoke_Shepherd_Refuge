# Album 59 - Yoke-Bearer &amp; Shepherd’s Refuge

Christian worship through clean animals (ox, gazelle, antelope, wild goat, ibex, addax, chamois) symbolizing redemption in New Jerusalem.

## Tracks:

Song 01 - The Ox's Yoke - Strong beast finding rest in Christ's light yoke.

Song 02 - The Gazelle's Leap - Swift grace and deliverance.

Song 03 - The Antelope's Freedom - Swift escape from darkness.

Song 04 - The Wild Goat's Refuge - Climbing to safety in God.

Song 05 - The Ibex's Climb - Steadfast ascent on the Rock.

Song 06 - The Addax's Song - Weary wanderer finding rest.

Song 07 - The Chamois's Praise - Tender folk ballad of guidance.

 ### Theology

 # 1. The Ox's Yoke — Strong beast finding rest in Christ's light yoke
A slow, resonant anthem that uses the ox as an emblem of strength laid down in humility. The music moves from heavy, laboring motifs into gentle, luminous textures as the ox accepts a new yoke—Christ’s light yoke—that converts burden into rest. Lyrically the song explores surrender, disciplined discipleship, and the paradox of power found in submission to the Good Shepherd.

• Biblical references: Matthew 11:28–30 (Christ’s yoke is easy and His burden light); Psalm 95:6–7 (bowing in worship); Isaiah 40:11 (the Shepherd caring for His flock); Galatians 5:1–6 (freedom in Christ through faith).

• Gospel message: Our labors and burdens do not save us, but in Christ we find rest and true strength. Jesus invites the weary to come, to take His yoke, and to learn from Him. Repent and trust in Christ—only He turns burden into rest and gives life that endures.

# 2. The Gazelle's Leap — Swift grace and deliverance
An energetic, dance-like piece that paints the image of a gazelle springing across ravines—alive, alert, delivered. Bright strings and agile percussion mirror the animal’s quick-footed grace, while lyrics celebrate sudden rescue and the joy of divine deliverance. The track pairs exhilaration with gratitude, moving from danger to exuberant praise.

• Biblical references: Psalm 18:33 (He makes my feet like the feet of a deer); Habakkuk 3:19 (God makes my feet like deer’s feet); 2 Samuel 22:34 (God trains my hands for war); Psalm 34:17–19 (the Lord delivers the righteous).

• Gospel message: God rescues those who turn to Him. The swift leap is an image of God’s immediate grace that delivers us from peril and sin. Trust Christ for deliverance: He rescues, restores, and sets our feet on a firm path to praise.

# 3. The Antelope's Freedom — Swift escape from darkness
A mid-tempo, liberating hymn that follows the antelope’s flight from shadow into open daylight. Atmospheric pads and driving rhythms represent spiritual momentum away from deception and bondage into freedom. The lyrics juxtapose the snares of darkness with the sunlit path of redemption found in Christ.

• Biblical references: John 8:31–36 (the truth sets you free); Colossians 1:13–14 (transferred from darkness into the kingdom of the Son); Romans 6:6–7, 14 (freedom from sin’s dominion); Isaiah 42:16 (leading out of darkness into light).

• Gospel message: Darkness and systems of bondage cannot hold those rescued by Christ. Through His life, death, and resurrection we are brought into freedom. Repent and believe—Christ’s light breaks every chain and leads into new life.

# 4. The Wild Goat's Refuge — Climbing to safety in God
A rugged, contemplative piece evoking broken cliffs and narrow ledges where the wild goat finds shelter. Sparse instrumentation—acoustic strings, woodwinds, and modal harmonies—creates a sense of precarious ascent that resolves into a secure summit. The song emphasizes God as refuge and the soul’s climb toward divine sanctuary.

• Biblical references: Psalm 46:1–3 (God is our refuge and strength); Psalm 61:2 (lead me to the rock that is higher than I); Psalm 18:2 (God is my rock and fortress); Hebrews 6:18–20 (we have a sure hope in Christ).

• Gospel message: When life’s cliffs threaten to overwhelm us, Christ is the refuge and sure foundation. He is the Rock to which we can cling. Come to Jesus in faith—He shelters, steadies, and secures those who seek Him.

# 5. The Ibex's Climb — Steadfast ascent on the Rock
 A majestic, steadily building canticle that chronicles the ibex’s patient, steadfast climb toward the mountain’s summit. Orchestration grows from solitary plucked strings into full choral arrival as the climber reaches the Rock. The lyrics dwell on perseverance, faith under trial, and the steadying presence of God during upward struggle.

• Biblical references: Psalm 121 (I lift up my eyes to the hills); Psalm 18:33 (He makes my feet like a deer’s); James 1:2–4 (testing produces steadfastness); Hebrews 12:1–3 (run with endurance the race set before us).

• Gospel message: Perseverance in faith is not mere human will but the work of God sustaining us. Christ secures the ascent—He bears our weakness and perfects our endurance. Trust Jesus moment by moment; He leads us safely to the summit of salvation.

# 6. The Addax's Song — Weary wanderer finding rest
A haunting, lullaby-like track for the wanderer: the addax whose long journey across desert wastes ends when it finds an oasis. Minimalist textures—soft harp, low winds, distant choir—create an atmosphere of weariness that yields to restorative peace. The lyrics focus on God’s provision for the weary and the promise of rest for the soul.

• Biblical references: Matthew 11:28–30 (come to Me, all who labor and are heavy laden); Psalm 23:1–3 (He leads me to green pastures, restores my soul); Isaiah 35:6–7 (the redeemed will sing for joy); Jeremiah 31:25 (I will satisfy the weary soul).

• Gospel message: The weary and wandering find true rest only in Christ. He invites us to lay down our burdens and enter His rest through repentance and faith. In Jesus we find renewal, provision, and everlasting comfort.

# 7. The Chamois's Praise — Tender folk ballad of guidance
A warm, intimate folk ballad centered on the chamois—nimble, shy, and attentive to guidance. Guitar and soft strings accompany a voice that testifies to gentle shepherding and personal guidance. The lyrics are devotional and pastoral, offering practical tenderness and the assurance of God’s daily leadings.

• Biblical references: Psalm 23 (the Lord is my shepherd); John 10:14–16 (I am the good shepherd); Psalm 25:4–5 (show me your ways, teach me your paths); Philippians 4:6–7 (present your requests to God and receive His peace).

• Gospel message: God’s guidance is personal, tender, and rooted in Christ the Good Shepherd. He knows each heart and calls us into relationship, daily care, and peace. Turn to Jesus; follow His voice—He leads us into life, purpose, and abiding joy.
